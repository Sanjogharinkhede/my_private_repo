Madhya Pradesh (/ˌmɑːdjə prəˈdɛʃ/;[9] Hindi: [ˈməd̪ʱjə pɾəˈd̪eːʃ] ⓘ; lit. 'Central Province') is a state in central India. Its capital is Bhopal, and its largest city is Indore. Other major cities of the state are Jabalpur and Gwalior. Madhya Pradesh is the second largest Indian state by area and the fifth largest state by population with over 72 million residents. It borders the states of Uttar Pradesh to the northeast, Chhattisgarh to the east, Maharashtra to the south, Gujarat to the west, and Rajasthan to the northwest.[10]
